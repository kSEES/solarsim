Planets

This is a list of all of the planets in the Orrery Space Model. The list contains information about all of the astronomically accepted planets and pluto as a dwarf planet.

Mercury

Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system, only being 1,516 mi in radius. This planet is not appropriate for organsims to live on due to its high temperatures and solar radiation. Temperatures can reach up to 800 degrees Fahrenheit. The surface of Mercury resembles Earth's Moon due to the craters and asteroids. It spins slowly on its axis.

Venus

Earth is the fifth largest planet in our solar system. This is the most hosipitable planet due to its temperature and chemicals. It completes one rotation every 24 hours and 365 days to complete a single trip. Earth has a single moon which helps keep the climate consistent. There are four layers that compose the Earth, those being the inner core, outer core, mantle, and crust.

Earth

Earth is the fifth largest planet in our solar system. This is the most hosipitable planet due to its temperature and chemicals. It completes one rotation every 24 hours and 365 days to complete a single trip. Earth has a single moon which helps keep the climate consistent. There are four layers that compose the Earth, those being the inner core, outer core, mantle, and crust.

Mars

Mars is the only planet that has had rover to roam around its landscape. These rovers are used to search for signs of life that may have existed previously. One rotation is completed 24.6 hours which is similar to Earth's rotation. Mars has two moons, Deimos and Phobos. They are shaped weirdly due to their lack of mass to make them into a spherical shape. The surface of Mars contains various colors such as brown, gold, and tan.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system. The temperatures of the planet are too extreme for organisms to adapt to. FIt is 11 times wider than Earth. One day on Jupiter is around 10 hours and one orbit around the sun is around 12 Earth years. Jupiter has four large moons and many smaller moons. In a way, Jupiter has its own mini solar system.

Saturn

Saturn is the second-largest planet in our solar system. It is made of helium and hydrogen. Similar to other planets, Saturns environment is not suitable for organisms to adapt to. One day on Saturn is around 11 hours and a complete orbit is around 29 Earth years. As of 2023, Saturn has 146 moons that are in orbit. Its rings are composed of small chunks of ice and rock that are coated with dust.

Uranus

Uranus has the third largest diameter of our solar system. One day on Uranus is around 17 hours and one complete orbit around the sun takes around 84 Earth years. Uranus contains 28 known moons. It's also four times wider than Earth. The environment is also not appropriate for life.

Neptune

Neptune is 30 times as far from the Sun as Earth. Neptune is also the only planet that cannot be viewed from the naked eye. One day on Neptune is around 16 hours and a complete orbit takes around 165 Earth years. There are 16 known moons that Neptune has. Neptune also contains five main rings and four ring arcs. Its atmosphere is composed of mostly hydrogen and helium.
